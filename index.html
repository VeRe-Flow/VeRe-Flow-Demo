VeRe-Flow: Flow Matching with Velocity Contrastive and Representation Regularization for Noise-Robust Bandwidth Expansion

Abstract

Noise-robust bandwidth expansion (NR-BWE) aims to reconstruct high-fidelity wideband speech from noisy low resolution inputs. Although flow matching has shown promising performance for speech generation, recovering clean signals under noisy conditions remains challenging. In this work, we reinforce clean signal learning within the flow matching framework through multi-level supervision. At the velocity level, we propose Velocity Contrastive Regularization (Vecor), which aligns the predicted velocity with the clean target trajectory while explicitly repelling it from the noisy velocity manifold, thereby enforcing separation in velocity space. To our knowledge, this is the first application of velocity-space contrastive supervision to speech generation. At the representation level, we introduce an auxiliary prediction head that aligns intermediate hidden states with clean self-supervised (SSL) speech representations, encouraging noise-invariant semantic encoding. We further enhance local modeling capacity with convolutional modules and incorporate noise-robust SSL features as conditioning. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed method improves perceptual quality, achieving higher mean opinion scores (MOS) and DNSMOS OVRL.

This work has been submitted to Interspeech 2026 for review.
